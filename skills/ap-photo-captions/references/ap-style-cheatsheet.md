# AP Style Cheat Sheet for Photo Captions

## Dates
- Day of week spelled out: Saturday
- Month abbreviated (except March, April, May, June, July): Feb. 21, 2026
- Full format: Saturday, Feb. 21, 2026

## Ages
- Always use numerals: "Jean Dupont, 63, a retiree"
- Set off with commas

## Titles
- Capitalize before name: President Macron
- Lowercase after name: Emmanuel Macron, president of France
- Lowercase standalone: the president

## Numbers
- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10 and above
- Always use numerals for ages, dates, addresses

## Locations
- City, Country: "in Lyon, France"
- Include state for U.S. cities not on the AP dateline list

## Names
- Full name on first reference: Fabienne Buccio
- Last name only on subsequent references: Buccio

## Photo-Specific
- Present tense for ongoing action: "holds," "marches," "addresses"
- Past tense for completed events referenced in context: "was fatally beaten"
- Identify left-to-right when multiple subjects: "From left, ..."
- Always translate foreign-language text visible in the photo
